# 📞 Cold Calling Local Businesses — Getting Your First Clients

The fastest way to turn this toolkit into money is to call local businesses that have a bad website or no website. This guide covers who to target, what to say, and how to close.

---

## Who to Target

Look for businesses with:
- No website at all (search Google Maps — some just have a listing)
- A website that looks like it was built in 2012
- A site that isn't mobile-friendly
- Only a Facebook page as their web presence

**Best sectors to start with:**

| Business Type | Why They're Good Targets |
|---|---|
| Hair salons / barbershops | Often have nothing, or a basic Facebook page |
| Local restaurants / cafés | Menus are PDFs, no online booking |
| Tradespeople (plumbers, electricians) | Zero web presence, rely on word of mouth |
| Gyms / personal trainers | Outdated site or just Instagram |
| Estate agents | Clunky legacy tools — animated redesign is a huge upgrade |
| Dentists / clinics | Sites from 2015, high-value clients who pay on time |
| Solicitors / accountants | Professional services, need credibility signals |
| Beauty salons / spas | Visual businesses — animated sites sell hard |

---

## Before You Call — Build a Mockup First

This is the single biggest conversion lever.

1. Find a business with a bad/no site
2. Spend 30–60 minutes building a demo of what their site could look like using Clo Design
3. Deploy it to your GitHub Pages (e.g. `yourusername.github.io/demo-salon`)
4. **Then** call them

Saying *"I've already started a mockup for you"* converts 3x better than *"I could build you something"*.

## The Call Scripts

### Opening (they pick up)

> "Hi, is that [Business Name]?"

> "Hi — my name's [Your Name], I'm a web designer based locally. I'm calling because I had a look at your website and I think there's a big opportunity to make it work a lot harder for you — I'm talking animated, professional-looking, and actually bringing in enquiries.
>
> I've been building sites for local businesses using some new AI tools — they look genuinely impressive and I can usually turn one around in a few days. Would it be worth a 10-minute call to show you what I mean?"

---

### "What does it cost?"

> "Depends on what you need — I usually start at around £800–£1,200 for a clean, modern animated site with everything set up. That includes the design, hosting sorted, and it being live on your domain.
>
> Some people want more pages or booking systems — that goes up a bit. But the honest answer is I'd rather show you a quick mockup first so you can actually see it before committing to anything."

---

### "We already have a website"

> "Totally understand — most businesses do. What I'm doing is a bit different though. I'm building sites with animated backgrounds, modern design, the kind of thing that makes people stop scrolling.
>
> Would you be open to me putting together a quick mockup of what yours could look like? Takes me an hour, costs you nothing, and you'd have something to compare against."

---

### "We're not interested"

> "No problem at all — I appreciate you taking the call. If you ever do want a refresh down the line, feel free to get in touch. Have a good one."

Don't push. Move on. Call the next one.

---

### "Send me something in an email"

> "Of course — what's the best address to send it to?"

Then follow up within 2 hours. Don't wait a day.

## Logistics

### How to find numbers
- **Google Maps** — search "[business type] [your town]", check their listing
- **Their website footer** — usually has a contact number
- **Facebook page** — "About" section often has a phone number
- **Walking the high street** — write down shop names, look up numbers later

### When to call
- **Tue–Thu, 10am–12pm or 2pm–4pm** — best pickup rates
- Avoid Mondays (everyone is in catch-up mode)
- Avoid Fridays (people are checked out)
- For restaurants: avoid meal times (12–2pm, 6–8pm)

### How many calls to expect to make
- 20 calls = ~5 conversations = ~2 interested = ~1 paying client
- This improves as you get better at the script and your mockups get stronger

## The Mockup Workflow (fast version)

1. Find target business
2. Look at their existing site (or note they have none)
3. Open Clo Design → build a 1-page version of what it could look like
4. Add their business name, a relevant animated background, their service list
5. Export → push to GitHub → get a live URL
6. Call → lead with *"I've got a mockup ready for you"*

Total time per mockup: 45–90 minutes once you're practiced.

---

## After the First Client

- Screenshot/record the finished site
- Post on Twitter: *"Built this for [Local Business] — animated site, live in 3 days"*
- Tag the business — they'll share it to their followers
- Add it to your portfolio on GitHub Pages
- Ask for a referral: *"Do you know any other local businesses who might benefit from this?"*

One happy client often leads to 2–3 more.

---

## Pro Tips

- **Lead with the demo, never the price.** Once they've seen it, 80% are sold before price comes up.
- **Walk your high street with a notepad.** Look for tatty signage, no web address on the window, or "find us on Facebook" — these are your targets.
- **Post your work publicly.** Social proof is a warm lead generator. Every post is a soft pitch.
- **Offer a free small change after delivery.** One round of tweaks. Builds goodwill and often leads to upsells.
- **Don't underprice.** Charging too little signals low quality. £800 is not expensive for a business that might spend £500/month on a yellow pages ad.
