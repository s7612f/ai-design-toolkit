# Getting Clients — Cold Calling Local Businesses

The fastest way to turn this toolkit into paying work is to call local businesses with bad or no websites. This guide is based on proven sales frameworks and real data from freelancers who've used this exact approach.

---

## Why this works

> "69% of buyers have accepted cold calls from new providers. Teams combining calls with email follow-up see a 70% lift in conversion compared to either channel alone."  
> — [Prospeo, *Cold Calling for Web Design Sales: Scripts That Work*](https://prospeo.io/s/cold-calling-web-design-sales)

> "Designers who commit consistently close 2–3 new projects per month. Expected conversion rate: 2–5% call-to-deal. At 50 calls/day over a month, that's roughly 2–3 closed projects worth £1,000–£2,500 each."  
> — [Callin, *Cold call script for website design in 2025*](https://callin.io/cold-call-script-for-website-design/)

Cold calling works for web design specifically because the problem is **visible before you call**. You can look at their site, identify exactly what's wrong, and lead with a specific observation rather than a generic pitch.

---

## Before you call — build a mockup first

This is the single biggest lever. Before calling a business, spend 45–90 minutes building a demo of their site using Clo Design or Claude Code.

Then your opening becomes: *"I've already started a mockup for you"* — not *"I could build you something."*

The difference in conversion is significant. You've already done the work, which demonstrates: you're serious, you're fast, and you can actually deliver. Objections about price or trust collapse faster when they can see something real.

**Mockup workflow (fast):**
1. Find a business with a bad site or no site
2. Screenshot their current site (or note they have none)
3. Build a 1-page version with Clo Design or Claude Code using their business name and services
4. Deploy it to your GitHub Pages or Cloudflare: `yourdomain.com/demo-[businessname]`
5. **Then** call them with a live URL ready to share

## Who to target

Look for businesses that match these signals:
- No website at all (check Google Maps — many just have a listing with no URL)
- Website hasn't been updated in years (look at copyright date in footer)
- Site doesn't work on mobile (open it on your phone)
- Only using Facebook/Instagram as their web presence
- High-value local services (anything where a client is worth £500+)

**Best sectors for first projects:**

| Business Type | Why They Convert |
|---|---|
| Hair salons / barbershops | Often have nothing, or a Facebook page with no updates |
| Local restaurants / cafés | Menus are PDFs from 2017. No online booking. Easy pitch. |
| Tradespeople (plumbers, electricians) | Zero web presence — rely entirely on word of mouth |
| Gyms / personal trainers | Outdated site or just Instagram. Visual business — Tier 2 is a strong sell |
| Dentists / clinics | Sites from 2015, professional services, pay reliably |
| Solicitors / accountants | Need credibility signals — exactly what a clean site provides |
| Florists / beauty salons | Visual businesses. Animated site is a huge upgrade from a static page |

**Avoid for your first few:** E-commerce, SaaS, anything that needs custom backend logic. Stick to service businesses with straightforward sites.

## When to call

> "Best calling windows: Tuesday through Thursday, 10am–12pm and 2pm–4pm local time. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons."  
> — [Callin, *Cold call script for website design in 2025*](https://callin.io/cold-call-script-for-website-design/)

For restaurants: avoid 12–2pm and 6–8pm (meal service).

**50 targeted calls beat 200 random dials.** Build a specific list from Google Maps and local directories — businesses in your town, in your target sectors, with visible website problems.

## The call scripts

These are based on documented sales frameworks — Grant Cardone's structure (use first name, ask for 30 seconds, state reason, qualify), and the "pattern interrupt" approach (identify a specific, visible problem before they can shut you down).

> "The most effective approach: create a psychological pattern interrupt by quickly identifying specific issues with the prospect's current website — e.g. 'mobile responsiveness limitations that are likely affecting your conversion rates.'"  
> — [Prospeo, *Cold Calling for Web Design Sales*](https://prospeo.io/s/cold-calling-web-design-sales)

---

### Opening script (they pick up)

> "Hi, is that [Business Name]?"  
>  
> "Hi — I'm [First Name]. Quick one — do you have 30 seconds?  
>  
> I'm a web designer based locally. I had a look at your site and there are a couple of things that are likely costing you enquiries — the main one is it's not working properly on mobile, which is where most people will find you.  
>  
> I've actually already started a mockup of what it could look like. Would it be worth me sending it over so you can see what I mean?"

**Why this works:**
- "30 seconds" is Cardone's approach — everyone has it and almost no one refuses
- You've identified a specific, verifiable problem (mobile) — not a generic pitch
- You've already done work — this triggers reciprocity

---

### If they have a website already

> "Totally understand — I've actually looked at it, which is why I called. The main issue I spotted is [specific thing: mobile layout / load speed / no call-to-action / outdated design].  
>  
> I've put together a quick mockup of what it could look like fixed. Would it be worth me sending it over? Costs you nothing to have a look."

---

### When they ask about price

**Don't quote cold.** First:

> "I'd rather show you the mockup before we talk numbers — once you can see it, the investment makes a lot more sense. But to give you a rough idea, most of what I do for local businesses starts around £800–£1,000 for a full site. Is that in the ballpark for you?"

> "I understand budget considerations are important. Most of my clients felt the same initially — but a site that's actually bringing in enquiries pays for itself quickly. That's what the mockup is for: I want to show you what's possible first."

> Reference: ["Budget objection response"](https://www.clari.com/blog/cold-calling-scripts-for-objection-handling/), Clari — *Cold Calling Scripts for Objection Handling*

---

### When they say "Not interested"

> "No problem at all — appreciate you picking up. If you ever do want a refresh down the line, feel free to get in touch."

Don't push. Hang up. Move on. One pleasant call where you leave well is worth more than a hard-sell that ends badly. They remember you positively, and people call back.

---

### When they say "Send me an email"

Get the address and send within 2 hours. Don't wait a day.

---

## Follow-up email template

**Subject:** Mockup for [Business Name]

> Hi [Name],  
>  
> As I mentioned — here's the mockup I put together: [link]  
>  
> Happy to walk through it on a quick call, 10 minutes. Let me know what works timing-wise.  
>  
> [Your name]  
> [Phone]

Keep it short. The link does the selling — not the email.

## Pricing

> "Cold calling is effective when project value is £1,000+. If projects are under £1,000, cold calling is not a good use of time."  
> — [Prospeo, *Cold Calling for Web Design Sales*](https://prospeo.io/s/cold-calling-web-design-sales)

| Package | Price | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | £800–£1,000 | 1-page site (Tier 1, Claude Code), hosted on Cloudflare/GitHub |
| **Full Site** | £1,200–£1,800 | 3–5 pages, mobile-optimised, custom domain setup |
| **Premium Animated** | £2,000–£2,800 | Tier 2 (Clo Design), animated backgrounds, liquid glass UI |
| **Retainer** | £150–£300/mo | Monthly updates, content changes, hosting management |

Adjust for your market. London rates run 20–30% higher than smaller towns.

**Don't underprice.** Charging too little signals low quality. A business spending £500/month on Google ads won't baulk at £1,000 for a site that converts those visitors.

## After closing — the next client comes from the first one

1. Deliver the site on time
2. Screenshot/record it and post on Twitter: *"Built this for [Local Business]"* — tag them
3. They share it to their followers → warm leads
4. Ask directly: *"Do you know any other local businesses who could use this?"*
5. One client typically leads to 2–3 referrals if you ask

> "Getting referrals is the most ideal way to land new clients — non-spammy, works even with just one past project under your belt."  
> — [Twine, *How to Get Web Design Clients as a Freelancer*](https://www.twine.net/blog/how-to-get-web-design-clients-as-a-freelancer/)

## What the data says about calling vs emailing

- 71% of cold emails get ignored
- 69% of buyers have accepted a cold call from someone new
- 6–8 touches needed to generate a viable lead — don't give up after one call

Best approach: **call first → follow-up email with the mockup link → call again 3 days later if no reply**. This multi-touch approach is what separates freelancers who get clients from those who don't.

> Source: [Better Proposals, *A Freelancer's Guide to Mastering Cold Outreach*](https://betterproposals.io/blog/cold-outreach-for-freelancers/)

## Finding numbers

- **Google Maps** — search "[business type] [your town]", check listing for phone number
- **Their website** — footer or contact page
- **Facebook business page** — "About" section usually has a phone number
- **Walking the high street** — notebook, spot tatty windows, no web address, "find us on Facebook" signs
