Build non-slop sites

What makes a site look professional vs cheap

These aren't opinions — they come from decades of usability research. The specific things to tell Claude or Clo Design to get a result that doesn't look AI-generated.

👁️

Hierarchy first

One goal per page. Every section serves it. Before you write a single prompt, answer: what's the one thing a visitor should do? If you can't, help the client figure it out.

Jakob Nielsen — "recognition over recall" is the most reliable predictor of user success. NN/g, 10 Usability Heuristics.

📐

Whitespace is the work

When a site feels busy and stressful, it's almost always a whitespace problem. Minimum 80px between sections. 24px internal card padding. Nothing cramped.

Smashing Magazine — "Intentional whitespace is a critical component of professional design." Gestalt Principles in Web Design.

🔤

Typography done right

Line height 1.5–1.6. Body text minimum 16px. Two fonts max. Dark grey on white (#1a1a1a on #fff) — not pure black. One font pair, used consistently.

Toptal — "Line spacing of 1.4 to 1.6 is the safe range." Designing for Readability: A Guide to Web Typography.

📱

Mobile is not optional

Most visitors will be on a phone. Build mobile-first. Test on a 375px screen. Hamburger menu. Touch targets 44px minimum. Always tell Claude this from the start.

A List Apart — "Responsive web design is the expected standard — not a bonus feature." Ethan Marcotte, 2010.

🔒

Trust signals

SSL. Working contact form. Real testimonials (placeholder first, client replaces them). Up-to-date footer copyright. These are the details that convert visitors into enquiries.

ThoughtMedia — "Professional sites show HTTPS, working forms, updated policies, testimonials. Cheap sites skip these details."

🎨

Consistent colour palette

2–3 colours maximum. One primary, one neutral, one accent. Changing colour palette every section is the fastest way to make a site look like a clearance sign.

Smashing Magazine — 10 Principles of Effective Web Design: "Changing colors every section makes a site feel cheap."

📞 Opening script

"Hi, is that [Business Name]?"

"Hi — I'm [First Name]. Quick one — do you have 30 seconds?"

"I'm a web designer based locally. I had a look at your site and there are a couple of things that are likely costing you enquiries — the main one is it's not working properly on mobile, which is where most people will find you."

"I've actually already started a mockup of what it could look like. Would it be worth me sending it over so you can see what I mean?"
Structure: Grant Cardone — use first name, ask for 30 seconds, state specific problem, low-pressure CTA.

💬 "We already have a website"

"Totally understand — I've actually looked at it, which is why I called. The main issue I spotted is [mobile layout / load speed / no CTA / outdated design]."

"I've put together a quick mockup of what it could look like fixed. Would it be worth me sending it over? Costs you nothing to have a look."
Pattern interrupt approach — identify a specific visible problem before they shut you down. Source: Prospeo.

💰 "What does it cost?"

"I'd rather show you the mockup first — once you can see it, the investment makes a lot more sense. But to give you a rough idea, most of what I do starts around £800–£1,000 for a full site."

"Most of my clients felt the same initially. A site that's actually bringing in enquiries pays for itself quickly — that's what the mockup is for."
Don't quote cold. Lead with the demo. Hormozi's CLOSER framework: address objections with ROI framing.

✉️ Follow-up email

Subject: Mockup for [Business Name]

"Hi [Name], as I mentioned — here's the mockup I put together: [link]"

"Happy to walk through it on a quick call, 10 minutes. Let me know what works timing-wise."

[Name] · [Phone]
Send within 2 hours of the call. Short — the link does the selling. Source: Jake Jorgovan, Better Proposals.

Inbound from Twitter/X

The post formula that gets 50K–300K views

Tier 2 animated sites are made for this. Build → record → post. Consistent inbound client leads from the feed.

Step 01

Post the tool stack, not the pitch

Use the format: "Clo Design + Kling 3.0" with your recording attached. Tool-stack posts get boosted by the tool companies' follower bases.

Step 02

CTA goes in the reply

Never pitch in the main post. Wait for traction, then reply: "I build these for clients. Starting at £[X]. Book a call →"

Step 03

Wrap it in Shots.so

Upload recording → pick an animated device mockup → export as MP4. About 10% visual quality boost for the post. Worth the 5 minutes.

Step 04

Post daily, repost others

Repost others who use your prompts. One post per day beats seven on Sunday. Quote trending AI announcements — algorithmic signal.

Clo Design + NanoBanana 2 + Kling 3.0 [attach your animated site recording] — then in the first reply once it's getting views — I build these for clients. Starting at £[X]. Book a call → [your link]
